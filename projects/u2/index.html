Nuclear Energy is Sustainable

Nuclear Energy

Most people believe that nuclear energy is dangerous, produces a lot of dangerous, radioactive waste, and don't understand its incredible consistency and efficiency. In reality, nuclear energy is significantly safer than other forms of power, significantly less waste is produced, and it is significantly more efficient.

Compare to Coal Power

Waste

Coal power plants produce about 333,000 tonnes of waste per year per plant compared to 37.7 tonnes for nuclear.[1] About 70% of coal waste is airborne ash and contains may toxic materials such as arsenic, lead, and mercury. Only some of this comparatively small amount is highly radioactive, needing storage casks. Despite popular belief, these casks are extremely safe and can store waste for decades.

Human Toll

Despite popular belief, nuclear energy is one of the safest energy sources available. Accidents such as Chernobyl and Fukushima, make it look extremely unsafe, however regulations across the world make accidents such as these practically impossible. Even with these accidents factored in, nuclear power resulted in just 0.03-0.04 deaths per TWh while coal resulted in at least 24.6 to 100 deaths per TWh. [2]

Reliability

Nuclear energy has a capacity factor of 92.5% compared to coal's 40.2%[3]. This means that nuclear power plants run 92.5% of each year continuously, refueling only every 1.5 to 2 years. This makes nuclear energy a great base load source, complementing the unpredictability of renewable sources.[4]
